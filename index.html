Why a walk in tub with shower can feel like the best of both worlds

Many families want the comfort of a deep, warm soak and the practicality of a quick shower. A well-designed combo balances both.
Homeowners & aging in place

Bath days and shower days in the same space

A traditional bathtub often forces a choice: climb over a tall wall for every shower, or give up soaking altogether for a step-in shower. A thoughtful walk in tub with shower gives you both, with a low threshold, a seat and a shower you can enjoy standing or sitting.

On days when joints feel stiff, you can sit, close the door and let warm water and hydrotherapy surround you. On busy mornings when you just want to rinse and go, you can use the shower quickly without filling the tub.

  • Low-threshold entry designed for more predictable movement in and out.
  • Chair-height seating that may feel easier on hips and knees than a floor-level tub.
  • Option to shower standing or seated with a handheld or mounted shower wand.
  • Space-saving design that fits where most traditional tubs used to sit.

Emotional benefits that matter just as much as features

When you or someone you love starts to worry about bathing, it is not only about surfaces and grab bars. It is about dignity, privacy and feeling like the bathroom is still a place of calm instead of stress.

Features to compare

Key decisions when choosing the best walk in tub shower combination

As you evaluate options, it helps to think in simple categories instead of getting lost in dozens of model names.

  • Threshold height: How high is the step to get through the door?
  • Seat height and depth: Does it feel natural to sit, stand and lean back?
  • Shower configuration: Handheld only, handheld + wall bar, or overhead rain-style?
  • Hydrotherapy: Air jets, water jets, both or a simple soaker tub?
  • Faucet placement: Can you reach controls comfortably while seated?
  • Drain and fill times: How will real-world water pressure and heater size affect use?

During your call, an Aging Safely Baths specialist can walk through these decisions in plain language and match you with a few strong candidates instead of a wall of confusing options.

    Frequently asked questions about walk in tub and shower combinations

    These are some of the questions seniors, caregivers and facilities ask us most often.

    Will a walk in tub with shower fit where my current bathtub is?

    In many homes, yes. Most walk in tub with shower systems are designed to fit in a standard alcove where a typical bathtub sits now. However, every bathroom is different. During a consultation, we look at photos, measurements and doorways to see how realistic the fit will be.

    Can I still take quick showers without filling the tub?

    Yes. One of the main reasons people choose a tub and shower combination is flexibility. You can use the shower alone for a quicker routine, or fill the tub for a full soak when you want warmth and hydrotherapy.

    How long does it take to fill and drain a walk in tub?

    Times vary depending on your home‚Äôs water pressure, plumbing and water heater size, as well as the specific tub model. During planning, we can talk about general ranges and ways to keep the experience as comfortable as possible.

    Do I have to use the jets every time?

    No. A walk in tub with shower that includes hydrotherapy can be used as a simple soaker tub whenever you want. Jets can typically be turned off or set on very gentle settings depending on how you feel that day.

    Is hydrotherapy okay for my health conditions?

    Only your doctor can answer this with confidence. We always encourage you to talk with your healthcare provider about warm water, soaking time and jet intensity. We can provide general product information that you can bring to your appointment.

    Will Medicare or insurance pay for a walk in tub and shower combination?

    Many traditional Medicare plans and insurance carriers do not view walk in tubs as covered medical equipment. However, some Medicare Advantage plans, local programs or veterans‚Äô benefits may offer partial assistance for accessibility upgrades.

    Can a walk in tub with shower work in a very small bathroom?

    Sometimes. There are compact models and front-entry designs that can help in tight spaces. The best first step is to measure your bathroom carefully and talk with us about door swings, clearances and any mobility devices you use.

    How do I clean and maintain the tub and shower?

    Most modern units are designed with smooth surfaces and accessible components to help with routine cleaning. Some hydrotherapy systems include cleaning cycles or sanitation features. We review maintenance instructions before and after installation.

    What if my loved one has memory changes or dementia?

    In these cases, simplicity is very important. We can talk about choosing a walk in tub shower combination with simpler controls, clear routines and support from caregivers so the experience feels predictable and calm.

    How long does installation usually take?

    Many projects can be completed within a few days once materials and schedules are aligned. More complex projects may take longer if plumbing or electrical changes are needed. We set realistic expectations so you are not left wondering what comes next.

    Can I use my own contractor?

    Yes. Aging Safely Baths can ship a walk in tub with shower directly to your home or contractor for DIY-style installation, as long as installation follows manufacturer guidelines. We can also coordinate turnkey projects if you prefer a single team handling everything.

    Do walk in tub and shower combinations help property value?

    Many buyers appreciate updated bathrooms and aging-in-place features. While no specific value increase can be guaranteed, thoughtful accessibility upgrades often make a home more attractive to a wider range of buyers.

    Can we try to reuse existing tile or finishes?

    In some projects, part of the surrounding wall finish can stay. In others, replacing the surround makes more sense. We talk through goals for appearance and budget before finalizing any plan.

    What brands do you carry?

    We partner with multiple leading walk in tub manufacturers and carefully select models that have performed well for our customers. Instead of pushing one brand, we match features with real-world needs and budgets.

    How do we get started without feeling rushed?

    A simple conversation is often the best first step. You do not have to decide anything on the first call. We ask questions, listen closely and then provide information you can review with your family and doctor.
